## 1. Global rules (apply first, site-wide)

### 1.1 Tone & voice
Rewrite copy across the whole site to this standard:
- **Positive, confident, inspiring.** Lead with what's *possible*, not what's broken.
- **Never imply the owner is failing or doing a bad job.** They've built something good; Indi adds firepower, it doesn't fix incompetence.
- **No fear, urgency, or "you're losing money" language.**
- **Respect the whole industry.** No negativity toward competitors, hotel chains, or OTAs. OTAs are strategic partners.
- **Keep a little empathetic tension** (e.g. "doing it all alone is hard") — honest and warm, never accusatory.
- Australian English. Sentence-case headlines.

### 1.2 Dashes
Remove em dashes, en dashes, and hyphens used as punctuation from all copy. Replace with a comma, or a full stop and a new sentence. Keep hyphens only in genuine compound words (e.g. check-in, back-of-house).

### 1.3 Chains & OTAs
Remove any language that says or implies Indi is "not a chain," or that reads as critical of chains or OTAs, everywhere. Reframe around the positives of independence with expert support (see About, §2.6).

### 1.4 Rate parity → "custom channel positioning"
Never present rate parity as the goal. Indi's position: every channel is priced with **intent**, and the direct channels (own website, phone, email) should always be the guest's best offer. Frame this as **strategy and direct-booking value**, never as working against OTA partners. Replace all "parity" language accordingly.

### 1.5 CTA rename: "Free Commercial Audit" → "Commercial Discovery"
Rename the primary CTA everywhere it appears (hero, buttons, closing bands, contact page, nav).
- Name: **Commercial Discovery** `[CONFIRM final name]`
- Button label: **"Start your free discovery"**
- Keep the reassurance "No cost, no commitment" where CTAs are described.

### 1.6 Design & brand
- **Logo:** use the **colour version (clay/brown sun)** everywhere — header, footer, favicon. Supply as SVG where possible. `[Sonny to provide final vector artwork]`
- **Mostly light site.** Backgrounds predominantly white / linen / paper. Dark sections are the rare exception.
- **The first (hero) section on every page must be light, not black.**
- **Buttons & primary accents use the warm logo tones — clay/brown (and teak).** Ocean (blue) and palm (green) only for small icons or fine details, used very sparingly.
- Type system unchanged: **Fraunces** (display), **Hanken Grotesk** (body), **IBM Plex Mono** (labels/data) — Google Fonts.
- Tokens: `--linen #F4EEE1 · --paper #F4F4F2 · --ink #1A1B1D · --stone #8C8070 · --clay #A9684A · --teak #C08B47 · --ocean #2E6E74 · --palm #5E6B4C`.

## 5. Image direction guide

**The principle:** don't photograph the work, photograph the world it lives in. No stock photos of people at laptops, offices, or desks with papers. Premium comes from **restraint** — fewer, better images, and generous white space.

**Sourcing priority (in order):**
1. **Real client properties** — beautiful shots of the independent hotels/motels Indi works with (a room, a facade at golden hour, a lobby detail, a coastal view). Authentic, on-brand, doubles as proof. *(Confirm rights/consent.)*
2. **Indi's own photography work** — use shots from real photography jobs (with permission). Proves the service while looking premium; the Digital Marketing / Projects pages especially.
3. **Curated atmospheric stock** — texture and light only, no faces, no offices. Prefer a paid, curated library (e.g. Stocksy) over generic free stock. Search: *boutique hotel interior, natural light, coastal Australia, linen texture, warm minimal interior, timber detail, morning light window.*
4. **Type & negative space** — where an image feels forced, use none. A bold Fraunces headline on warm white space reads more premium than any photo.

**Per slot:**
- **Homepage hero:** one atmospheric wide shot of a beautiful independent property at golden hour — or no photo at all (logo + headline + warm space). Confident either way.
- **Capability pages:** one abstract/detail image each (a calm interior, a coastal view, a warm material detail) — not literal illustrations of the concept.
- **About:** the **one place a real human photo belongs.** A warm, natural, well-lit portrait of Sonny / the team (even if remote, composited tastefully). A genuine small-family-business photo beats any stock handshake.
- **Results:** the client properties themselves — this is where they shine.
- **Projects:** Indi's own photography work as examples.

**Remove rather than fill:** where a placeholder doesn't have a strong, on-brand image available, **delete the image slot and let white space carry the section.** Flag these to Sonny.

**Do / Don't:**
- Do: natural light, warm tones, real places, consistent mood (all images should feel shot in the same warm, natural register).
- Don't: stock office/people-at-laptop imagery, glossy "luxury lobby" clichés, mismatched lighting/tone, or filling every slot for the sake of it. Four beautiful images beat twelve mediocre ones.
