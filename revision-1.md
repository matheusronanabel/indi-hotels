## A. Global rules (apply site-wide)

1. **OTA tone — important.** OTAs are valued strategic partners. Remove any language
    that's critical of them or frames the goal as "reducing reliance on OTAs," "OTAs take their
    cut," "not OTA traffic," etc. Reframe around the owner's positive outcomes instead: **grow**
    **revenue, build direct market share, lower cost of guest acquisition, balance the**
    **channel mix.** Sweep the whole site for OTA-negative phrasing, not just the instances
    listed below.
2. **Use "bespoke."** Indi's support is tailored to each property — every hotel is different.
    Favour words like _bespoke_ , _built around your property_ , _tailored_. Avoid "one strategy" /
    "the same strategy for everyone."
3. **Mostly light.** The site should read predominantly light — white, linen, paper. Dark
    sections are now rare, not the default (see Design §D2–D3).
4. **Accent colours follow the logo.** Lead accents are the warm logo tones — **clay/brown**
    **and teak (the "orange/brown")** — used for buttons and key accents. **Ocean (blue) and**
    **palm (green) are used very sparingly** (small icons/details only).

## D. Design changes

**D1 — Logo: use the colour version.** Switch the logo from the mono/black version to the
**two-tone version with the clay/brown sun circle** , everywhere (header, footer, favicon/app
icon). Wordmark colour adapts to background (ink on light, paper on dark).
**D2 — First section on each page must not be black.** The top/hero container currently uses
the dark (near-black) background. Change the first section on every page to a **light
background** — linen/paper/white, or a soft tint — with ink text. (This pairs with D3: the site
should feel light.)
**D3 — Light site, warm accents.**

- Backgrounds predominantly **white / linen / paper** ; dark sections become the rare
    exception.
- **Buttons and primary accents use the warm logo tones — clay/brown (and teak)** —
    so the UI matches the logo.
       - Suggested: primary button = **clay #A9684A** , white text; hover = a deeper clay (≈
          #8F5639). Secondary button = outline in ink, or teak #C08B47.
- **Ocean (blue) #2E6E74 and palm (green) #5E6B4C: very sparing only** — small icons
    or fine details, never buttons or fields.
- Keep the type system unchanged (Fraunces / Hanken Grotesk / IBM Plex Mono) and the
    overall restraint (generous whitespace, one accent per view).
Note: D1–D3 shift the brand's accent rules (warm-led and light, rather than
ocean-led with dark heroes). The **style guide should be updated to match** so it
stays the single source of truth — see message accompanying this brief.
